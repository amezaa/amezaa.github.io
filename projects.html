RobotX is an autonomous marine robotics competition held every two years. As part of the MIT RobotX team I'm one of two people on the Computer Vision subteam, in charge of getting the remote explorer to take in camera data to identify locations and various objects types, shapes, and colors.

Fire Response Safety App

I was inspired to make this during HackMIT 2018, with the intent of participating IBM's Code for Good challenge. The idea is that you have people report a fire by taking an image of it. The app will record the user's location and direction, and alert nearby users of a possible fire. Nearby users can then check around them to see if they see the same fire. That user can then repeat the process. With enough people it should be possible to triangulate the position of the fire and alert first responders. This was my first time trying to code it app, and it turns out learn app-dev in one day is definitely a challenge. But with a little more debugging, the app should be functional.

Drone Fire Prevention [In progress]

I'm tired of seeing my state (California) constantly on fire. This inspired the idea to use a drone with a go-pro camera attached to identify potential fire prone areas; the goal being smarter and fast fire prevention. The identification process will use a CNN trained on images of fire-prone sources to analyze the camera data, with the potential to add more sensors in the future to have more data that could identify fire prone areas.
